üéØ Risk Treatment Options

üõ°Ô∏è Mitigate

Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact. Most common approach for critical/high risks.

üîÑ Transfer

Use insurance or contracts to shift risk. Consider for high-impact, low-frequency events.

‚úÖ Accept

Acknowledge risk but take no action. Only for risks within appetite threshold.

üö´ Avoid

Eliminate risk by changing business process. Consider for unacceptable risks.

üìã Risk Register Template

Your risk register should include these elements for each identified risk:

  • Risk ID: Unique identifier (R001, R002, etc.)
  • Risk Description: Clear statement of the risk scenario
  • Asset(s) Affected: Which critical assets are at risk
  • Threat Source: What or who poses the threat
  • Vulnerability: Weakness that could be exploited
  • Likelihood: Probability of occurrence (1-5 scale)
  • Impact: Business consequence if risk occurs (1-5 scale)
  • Risk Level: Calculated risk score (Likelihood √ó Impact)
  • Treatment Decision: Mitigate, Transfer, Accept, or Avoid
  • Control Measures: Existing or planned risk controls
  • Owner: Person responsible for managing the risk
  • Timeline: Target dates for risk treatment actions

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Risk Assessment Pitfalls

  • Underestimating Impact: Consider all costs including reputation, legal, recovery
  • Overestimating Likelihood: Base on actual data, not fear or headlines
  • Ignoring Interdependencies: Map how asset failures cascade to other systems
  • Static Assessment: Risk changes constantly, build in regular review cycles
  • Generic Treatment Plans: Ensure controls address specific identified risks
  • Poor Communication: Translate technical risks into business language
