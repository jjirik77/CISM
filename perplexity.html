Domain 1: Information Security Governance
17% of Exam

ðŸŽ¯ Domain Overview

This domain provides thorough insight into culture, regulations and structure involved in enterprise governance, enabling you to analyze, plan and develop information security strategies with high-level credibility to stakeholders.

A: Enterprise Governance
  • Organizational Culture

    Understanding how organizational culture impacts security governance, including risk tolerance, communication patterns, and decision-making processes.

  • Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Requirements

    Compliance with laws like SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, and industry standards that impact information security governance frameworks.

  • Organizational Structures, Roles and Responsibilities

    Defining clear accountability for security governance, including board oversight, executive sponsorship, and operational responsibilities.

B: Information Security Strategy
  • Information Security Strategy Development

    Creating comprehensive security strategies aligned with business objectives, risk appetite, and regulatory requirements.

  • Information Governance Frameworks and Standards

    Implementing frameworks like NIST, ISO 27001, COBIT to structure governance processes and ensure consistent security practices.

  • Strategic Planning (Budgets, Resources, Business Case)

    Developing business cases for security investments, budget planning, resource allocation, and ROI measurement for security initiatives.

ðŸ”‘ Key Terms & Concepts

ERM (Enterprise Risk Management)

Integrated approach to managing all organizational risks, including information security risks.

COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies)

Framework for IT governance and management, providing guidance for enterprise IT governance.

KGI (Key Goal Indicator)

Metrics that measure achievement of security goals and strategic objectives.

Domain 2: Information Security Risk Management
20% of Exam

ðŸŽ¯ Domain Overview

This domain empowers you to analyze and identify potential information security risks, threats and vulnerabilities, providing comprehensive knowledge for performing risk management at the management level.

A: Information Security Risk Assessment
  • Emerging Risk and Threat Landscape

    Staying current with evolving cyber threats, attack vectors, and emerging technologies that introduce new security risks.

  • Vulnerability and Control Deficiency Analysis

    Systematic identification and assessment of security weaknesses in systems, processes, and controls.

  • Risk Assessment and Analysis

    Quantitative and qualitative methods for evaluating risk likelihood, impact, and overall risk exposure to the organization.

B: Information Security Risk Response
  • Risk Treatment / Risk Response Options

    Four primary risk treatment strategies: Accept, Avoid, Mitigate, and Transfer. Selection based on risk tolerance and cost-benefit analysis.

  • Risk and Control Ownership

    Establishing clear accountability for risk management and control implementation across the organization.

  • Risk Monitoring and Reporting

    Continuous monitoring of risk posture, control effectiveness, and regular reporting to stakeholders and management.

ðŸ”‘ Key Terms & Concepts

Risk Appetite

The amount and type of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of business objectives.

Risk Tolerance

The acceptable level of variation in outcomes related to specific performance measures linked to objectives.

Inherent Risk

The level of risk present before any controls or mitigation measures are applied.

Residual Risk

The remaining risk after security controls and mitigation measures have been implemented.

Domain 3: Information Security Program
33% of Exam

ðŸŽ¯ Domain Overview

This domain covers resources, asset classifications and frameworks for information security, empowering you to manage comprehensive information security programs including security controls, testing, communications, reporting and implementation.

A: Information Security Program Development
  • Information Security Program Resources

    Planning and allocating people, tools, technologies, and budget required for effective security program implementation.

  • Information Asset Identification and Classification

    Systematic inventory and categorization of information assets based on value, sensitivity, and criticality to business operations.

  • Industry Standards and Frameworks

    Leveraging established frameworks like ISO 27001, NIST CSF, CIS Controls to structure security program development.

  • Information Security Policies, Procedures and Guidelines

    Creating comprehensive documentation hierarchy to guide security implementation and ensure consistent practices.

  • Information Security Program Metrics

    Establishing KPIs and KRIs to measure program effectiveness, efficiency, and alignment with business objectives.

B: Information Security Program Management
  • Information Security Control Design and Selection

    Choosing appropriate security controls based on risk assessments, compliance requirements, and business needs.

  • Information Security Control Implementation and Integration

    Deploying security controls effectively while ensuring integration with existing business processes and systems.

  • Information Security Control Testing and Evaluation

    Regular assessment of control effectiveness through testing, monitoring, and validation activities.

  • Information Security Awareness and Training

    Developing and maintaining comprehensive security awareness programs to educate and engage employees.

  • Management of External Services

    Overseeing security aspects of third-party providers, suppliers, and other external parties with access to organizational resources.

  • Information Security Program Communications and Reporting

    Regular communication of security program status, metrics, and issues to stakeholders and management.

ðŸ”‘ Key Terms & Concepts

Security Architecture

The design and structure of security controls within an organization's overall enterprise architecture.

Defense in Depth

Layered security approach using multiple security controls to protect information assets.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator)

Metrics that measure the performance and effectiveness of security program activities.

KRI (Key Risk Indicator)

Forward-looking metrics that provide early warning of increasing risk exposure.

Domain 4: Incident Management
30% of Exam

ðŸŽ¯ Domain Overview

This domain provides in-depth training in incident management readiness and operations, including preparation for incident response, business recovery guidance, and comprehensive incident management tools and techniques.

A: Incident Management Readiness
  • Incident Response Plan

    Comprehensive documented procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

    Assessment of potential impacts from security incidents on business operations, including financial, operational, and reputational effects.

  • Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

    Strategic plan ensuring critical business functions can continue during and after a security incident or disaster.

  • Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

    Technical procedures for restoring IT systems and infrastructure following a significant incident or disaster.

  • Incident Classification/Categorization

    Systematic approach to classifying incidents by severity, type, and impact to enable appropriate response procedures.

  • Incident Management Training, Testing and Evaluation

    Regular training programs, tabletop exercises, and simulations to ensure incident response team readiness.

B: Incident Management Operations
  • Incident Management Tools and Techniques

    Security information and event management (SIEM) systems, forensic tools, and automated response technologies.

  • Incident Investigation and Evaluation

    Systematic investigation processes to determine incident scope, impact, root cause, and attribution.

  • Incident Containment Methods

    Immediate actions to limit incident spread and minimize damage, including isolation and quarantine procedures.

  • Incident Response Communications

    Internal and external communication protocols for reporting, notification, and escalation during incident response.

  • Incident Eradication and Recovery

    Procedures for eliminating incident causes and restoring normal operations while preventing recurrence.

  • Post-Incident Review Practices

    Comprehensive analysis following incident resolution to identify lessons learned and improve future response capabilities.

ðŸ”‘ Key Terms & Concepts

RTO (Recovery Time Objective)

The maximum acceptable length of time that a system can be down after a failure or disaster occurs.

RPO (Recovery Point Objective)

The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time before the disaster occurs.

CSIRT (Computer Security Incident Response Team)

Dedicated team responsible for coordinating and managing incident response activities.

Forensic Analysis

Scientific examination and analysis of digital evidence to support incident investigation and potential legal proceedings.
