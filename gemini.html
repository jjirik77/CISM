Why Pursue the CISM Certification?

The Certified Information Security Manager (CISM) certification, issued by ISACA, is a premier credential for professionals managing and overseeing information security systems in alignment with business goals. Focused on governance, risk management, and leadership, CISM is ideal for security managers, CISOs, and consultants who drive security strategies across the enterprise. Click on the cards below to explore the key benefits.

Leadership-Focused

Develop and enhance your skills in leading information security teams and initiatives.

Career-Enhancing

Boost your professional credibility and open doors to advanced roles in the cybersecurity field.

Global Recognition

Gain a globally recognized certification that validates your expertise to employers worldwide.

Industry Relevance

Stay current with the latest trends and best practices in information security management.

CISM Exam Details

Everything you need to know about the CISM exam, from its format and cost to the experience required and testing options.

Exam Format & Domains +
  • Format: 150 multiple-choice questions
  • Duration: 4 hours (240 minutes)
  • Passing Score: 450 out of 800 (scaled score)
Exam Domains

Click each domain to learn more about its focus area.

Domain 1: Governance

Establish and maintain an information security governance framework and supporting processes to ensure alignment with organizational goals.

Domain 2: Risk Management

Manage information risk to an acceptable level based on the organization's risk appetite to meet its goals and objectives.

Domain 3: Program Development

Develop and maintain an information security program that identifies, manages, and protects the organization’s assets.

Domain 4: Incident Management

Plan, establish, and manage the capability to detect, investigate, respond to, and recover from information security incidents.

Experience & Membership Requirements +

Experience Required:
A minimum of 5 years of professional information security management work experience is required for certification.

  • 3 of those years must be in at least 3 of the 4 CISM domains.
  • Substitutions for up to 2 years of general experience are available based on qualifying certifications (like CISSP, CISA) or a postgraduate degree in a related field.

ISACA Membership (Optional):
While not mandatory, ISACA membership offers significant benefits, including exam discounts, free webinars, access to research, and networking opportunities with a global community of professionals.

Where to Take the Exam +

ISACA offers flexible testing options to accommodate candidates worldwide. You can schedule your exam year-round through the official ISACA website.

  • Remote Proctored Online Exam: Take the exam from the comfort of your home or office. This option is available through both PSI and Pearson VUE platforms, monitored by a live proctor via webcam.
  • In-Person Testing Centers: For those who prefer a traditional testing environment, the exam is offered at Pearson VUE testing centers located globally.

Visit www.isaca.org to find a testing center or schedule your remote exam.

Maintaining Your CISM Certification

Holding the CISM certification is an ongoing commitment to professional development. To maintain your credential, you must adhere to ISACA's requirements for continuing professional education (CPE) and professional conduct.

CPE Requirements

You must demonstrate your commitment to keeping your skills current by earning and reporting Continuing Professional Education (CPE) credits.

Yearly CPE Requirements

Earn a minimum of 20 CPE credits annually.

Cycle CPE Requirements

Earn a total of 120 CPE credits over a 3-year reporting cycle.

Annual Maintenance Fee

An annual fee is required to maintain your certification in good standing.

ISACA Member $45 USD

Code of Professional Ethics

Adherence to the ISACA Code of Professional Ethics is mandatory. Click each principle to learn more.

Principle 1

Support the implementation of, and encourage compliance with, appropriate standards and procedures for information systems.

Principle 2

Perform duties with objectivity, due diligence, and professional care, in accordance with professional standards.

Principle 3

Serve the interest of stakeholders in a lawful and honest manner, while maintaining high standards of conduct.

Principle 4

Maintain the privacy and confidentiality of information obtained in the course of your duties unless disclosure is required by law.

Principle 5

Maintain competency in respective fields and agree to undertake only those activities that can be reasonably expected to be completed with professional competence.

Principle 6

Inform appropriate parties of the results of work performed, including the disclosure of all significant facts that, if not disclosed, may distort the reporting of results.

Principle 7

Support the professional education of stakeholders in enhancing their understanding of the governance and management of enterprise information systems.
