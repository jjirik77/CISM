Overview

Objective

To apply the core concepts of Information Security Governance (Domain 1) and Information Risk Management (Domain 2) in a realistic, collaborative scenario designed for a remote learning environment.

Phase 1: Setup & Role Assignment

Student Instructions

Welcome to the Nexus Health Corp. challenge! You are now in your breakout room. Your first task is to choose your roles for this exercise. Read through the role cards below and decide as a team who will play each part. One person should be designated to take notes in your group's shared document.

Role Cards

Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

You are ultimately responsible for the platform's security. Your goal is to manage risk effectively while enabling the business to succeed.

Head of Product Development

You must launch "ConnectWell" on time and on budget. You worry that excessive security will cause delays and create a poor user experience.

General Counsel (Legal)

Your priority is compliance with HIPAA and GDPR. A data breach would be catastrophic, leading to massive fines and reputational damage.

Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

You control the budget. Every security expense must be justified with a clear ROI. You are focused on the bottom line and the company's risk appetite.

Lead Risk Analyst

You are the CISO's right hand. Your job is to identify threats, analyze their impact using qualitative and quantitative methods, and recommend treatment options.

Phase 2: The Challenge

Your Team's Mission:

As a team, use your shared document to work through the two parts of the challenge below. Use the menu on the left to access the Scenario Brief and Supporting Documents to inform your decisions.

Part 1: Governance and Strategy (15 minutes)

  1. Establish the "Why": Draft a simple, one-sentence **Information Security Policy** statement for the ConnectWell platform.
  2. Define Roles: Fill out the **RACI Matrix** for the task: "Approving third-party access to patient data within the ConnectWell platform."

Part 2: Risk Management (20 minutes)

Your Lead Risk Analyst has identified a critical risk related to the "Family Share" feature. Analyze it and decide how to respond.

  1. Qualitative Analysis: Assign a "High," "Medium," or "Low" rating for both the **Likelihood** and **Impact** of this risk. Justify your ratings.
  2. Quantitative Analysis: Using the figures in the Supporting Documents, calculate the **SLE** and **ALE**.
  3. Risk Response Decision: Choose one of the three proposed options (Mitigate, Transference, or Accept) and write a justification for your choice.

Phase 3: Debrief & Discussion

Group Discussion

The facilitator will now lead a discussion with the entire class. Be prepared to share your screen and present your group's findings and decisions.

Discussion Questions

  • How did your assigned roles influence your decisions? Was there conflict between the CFO's budget concerns and the General Counsel's compliance focus?
  • Which was more helpful in making your decision: the qualitative or the quantitative analysis? Why?
  • How did the company's stated **risk appetite** affect your final choice?
  • What are the potential consequences of each of the three risk response options you didn't choose?

Scenario Brief

Nexus Health Corp. is a fast-growing health technology company. Your team is about to launch a new flagship product: "ConnectWell," a cloud-based platform that allows patients to access their medical records, communicate with doctors, and share data with family members. The platform will store Protected Health Information (PHI) and will be marketed to customers in both the United States and the European Union.

The Board of Directors has set an aggressive launch date (6 months away) and has defined the company's risk appetite as "moderate," willing to accept some risks for innovation but intolerant of any that could lead to major legal violations or reputational harm.

Identified Risk

The platform's "Family Share" feature allows patients to grant access to their health records to family members. However, there is no strong identity verification process for those family members.

  • Threat: A malicious actor could impersonate a family member to gain unauthorized access to a patient's sensitive health records.
  • Vulnerability: Weak identity verification for non-patient users.

Supporting Documents

INTERNAL MEMO: Board of Directors

Subject: Risk Appetite for the ConnectWell Initiative

The Board has approved a "moderate" risk appetite for the ConnectWell platform. We encourage innovation and speed to market. However, the Board is intolerant of any risks that could result in significant regulatory fines (HIPAA, GDPR) or cause lasting damage to the Nexus Health brand. Financial prudence is expected in all mitigation efforts.

WORKSHEET: Lead Risk Analyst

Subject: Quantitative Analysis for "Family Share" Vulnerability

Metric Value Notes
Asset Value $5,000,000 Estimated cost of fines and reputational damage from a single major breach.
Exposure Factor (EF) 20% A breach of this type would likely result in a partial loss.
Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO) 0.5 An incident of this type is likely to occur once every two years.

PROPOSAL: Solutions Team

Subject: Risk Response Options for "Family Share" Vulnerability

Option A (Mitigate)

Implement a multi-factor authentication (MFA) and identity verification service for all "Family Share" users. This will significantly reduce the risk.

Cost: $80,000 per year.

Option B (Transference)

Purchase a specialized cyber insurance policy that would cover the financial losses from this specific type of breach. This does not reduce the likelihood but transfers the financial impact.

Cost: $50,000 per year.

Option C (Accept)

Launch with the current design to meet the deadline. Acknowledge the risk in a risk register and plan to address it in a future update.

Cost: $0 (initially).
