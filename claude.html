CISM Certification Overview

Certified Information Security Manager

Why Pursue the CISM Certification?

The Certified Information Security Manager (CISM) certification, issued by ISACA, is a premier credential for professionals managing and overseeing information security systems in alignment with business goals. Focused on governance, risk management, and leadership, CISM is ideal for security managers, CISOs, and consultants who drive security strategies across the enterprise.

Information Security Governance

17% of Exam

This domain focuses on developing, maintaining, and managing information security governance frameworks aligned with organizational objectives and business strategy.

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Governance ensures that security strategies align with business objectives and comply with applicable laws and regulations. It provides strategic direction for cybersecurity activities and ensures organizational security objectives are effectively met through proper oversight and accountability.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

A. Enterprise Governance

  • Organizational Culture: Understanding how culture impacts security strategy implementation and adoption
  • Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Requirements: Compliance with laws, regulations, and contractual obligations
  • Organizational Structures, Roles and Responsibilities: Defining clear security roles and accountability frameworks
  • Enterprise Risk Management Integration: Aligning security governance with overall enterprise risk management
  • Board and Executive Engagement: Securing leadership commitment and providing proper oversight

B. Information Security Strategy

  • Information Security Strategy Development: Creating comprehensive security strategies aligned with business objectives
  • Information Governance Frameworks and Standards: Implementing frameworks like ISO 27001, NIST, COBIT
  • Strategic Planning: Budget allocation, resource planning, and business case development
  • Performance Measurement: Defining KPIs and metrics for governance effectiveness
  • Stakeholder Communication: Reporting and communicating governance activities and outcomes

üéØ Key Supporting Tasks

  • Identify internal and external influences on information security strategy
  • Establish and maintain information security strategy aligned with organizational goals
  • Establish and maintain information security governance framework
  • Integrate information security governance into corporate governance
  • Establish and maintain information security policies
  • Develop business cases to support information security investments
  • Gain ongoing commitment from senior leadership and stakeholders

Information Security Risk Management

20% of Exam

This domain empowers you to analyze and identify potential information security risks, threats, and vulnerabilities, providing comprehensive risk management capabilities for organizational protection.

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Risk Management focuses on identifying, assessing, and treating information security risks to acceptable levels based on organizational risk appetite. It encompasses both strategic and operational risk management activities, ensuring comprehensive protection of organizational assets.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

A. Information Security Risk Assessment

  • Emerging Risk and Threat Landscape: Identifying new and evolving threats and vulnerabilities
  • Vulnerability and Control Deficiency Analysis: Systematic identification and analysis of weaknesses
  • Risk Assessment and Analysis: Quantitative and qualitative risk assessment methodologies
  • Risk Identification Techniques: Asset-based, threat-based, and vulnerability-based approaches
  • Business Impact Analysis: Understanding potential impacts of security incidents on operations

B. Information Security Risk Response

  • Risk Treatment/Risk Response Options: Accept, avoid, transfer, or mitigate risk strategies
  • Risk and Control Ownership: Defining clear accountability for risk management activities
  • Risk Monitoring and Reporting: Continuous monitoring and stakeholder communication processes
  • Risk Appetite and Tolerance: Defining and communicating acceptable levels of risk
  • Control Selection and Implementation: Choosing and deploying appropriate risk treatment measures

üéØ Key Supporting Tasks

  • Participate in and oversee risk identification, assessment, and treatment processes
  • Participate in and oversee vulnerability assessment and threat analysis
  • Identify and implement appropriate risk treatment options
  • Determine effectiveness of information security controls
  • Facilitate integration of information risk management into business processes
  • Monitor for factors requiring risk reassessment
  • Report on information security risk to key stakeholders

Information Security Program

33% of Exam

This domain covers implementing information security strategies and developing comprehensive security programs, guidelines, procedures, and metrics. This is the highest-weighted domain in the CISM exam.

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Program Management focuses on translating security strategy into operational reality through program development, implementation, and management. It encompasses the practical aspects of building and maintaining effective security programs that deliver measurable results.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

A. Information Security Program Development

  • Information Asset Identification and Classification: Data classification schemes and comprehensive asset inventory

  • Information Security Program Metrics: KPIs and measurement frameworks for program effectiveness

B. Information Security Program Management

  • Information Security Control Design and Selection: Choosing and designing appropriate security controls
  • Information Security Control Implementation and Integration: Deploying controls across the organization
  • Information Security Control Testing and Evaluation: Assessing control effectiveness and performance
  • Information Security Awareness and Training: Education programs and security culture development
  • Management of External Services: Third-party, fourth-party, and vendor risk management
  • Information Security Program Communications and Reporting: Stakeholder communication and reporting frameworks

üéØ Key Supporting Tasks

  • Align information security program with operational objectives
  • Establish and maintain information security processes and resources
  • Establish, communicate, and maintain organizational security documentation

  • Integrate security requirements into organizational processes
  • Integrate security requirements into external party contracts
  • Monitor external parties' adherence to security requirements
  • Define and monitor management and operational metrics

Information Security Incident Management

30% of Exam

This domain provides comprehensive training in incident management, including preparedness, response, recovery, and continuous improvement processes for organizational resilience.

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Incident Management encompasses all activities related to preparing for, responding to, and recovering from security incidents. It ensures organizational resilience through effective incident response capabilities and continuous improvement of security posture.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

A. Incident Management Readiness

  • Incident Response Plan: Comprehensive planning for incident response activities and procedures
  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): Understanding critical business functions and dependencies
  • Business Continuity Plan (BCP): Ensuring business operations continue during and after incidents
  • Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP): Restoring IT systems and operations after incidents
  • Incident Classification/Categorization: Systematic approach to incident triage and prioritization
  • Incident Management Training, Testing and Evaluation: Preparedness through exercises and training programs

B. Incident Management Operations

  • Incident Management Tools and Techniques: Technology and methodologies for effective incident response
  • Incident Investigation and Evaluation: Forensic analysis and evidence preservation procedures
  • Incident Containment Methods: Strategies to limit incident impact and prevent spread
  • Incident Response Communications: Internal and external communication protocols and procedures
  • Incident Eradication and Recovery: Removing threats and restoring normal business operations
  • Post-Incident Review Practices: Lessons learned and continuous improvement processes

üéØ Key Supporting Tasks

  • Establish and maintain incident response plan aligned with BCP and DRP
  • Establish and maintain incident classification and categorization process
  • Develop processes for timely identification of security incidents
  • Establish processes to investigate and document incidents per legal requirements
  • Establish incident handling processes including containment and recovery
  • Organize, train, equip, and assign responsibilities to incident response teams
  • Establish incident communication plans for internal and external parties
  • Evaluate incident management plans through testing and exercises
  • Conduct post-incident reviews for continuous improvement
