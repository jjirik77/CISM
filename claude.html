üéØ Detailed Sub-domains and Topics

A. Enterprise Governance

Enterprise governance provides the foundation for all information security activities. It establishes the organizational context, culture, and structure within which security operates.

üè¢ Organizational Culture

Organizational culture significantly impacts security strategy implementation and adoption. Understanding and working with cultural factors is essential for successful security governance.

Key Cultural Factors:
  • Risk Tolerance: Organization's appetite for risk affects security investment decisions and control implementation
  • Communication Styles: Formal vs. informal communication patterns influence security messaging and policy adoption
  • Decision-Making Processes: Centralized vs. decentralized decision-making affects security authority and accountability
  • Change Management: Organizational readiness for change impacts security transformation initiatives
  • Innovation vs. Control: Balance between encouraging innovation and maintaining security controls
üí° CISM Insight: Successful CISMs adapt their governance approach to align with organizational culture rather than fighting against it. This includes tailoring communication styles, risk assessment approaches, and control implementation strategies to fit the cultural context.

Information Security Risk Management

20% of Exam

This domain empowers you to analyze and identify potential information security risks, threats, and vulnerabilities, providing comprehensive risk management capabilities for organizational protection.

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Risk Management focuses on identifying, assessing, and treating information security risks to acceptable levels based on organizational risk appetite. It encompasses both strategic and operational risk management activities, ensuring comprehensive protection of organizational assets.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

A. Information Security Risk Assessment

  • Emerging Risk and Threat Landscape: Identifying new and evolving threats, attack vectors, threat intelligence, and emerging technologies risks
  • Vulnerability and Control Deficiency Analysis: Systematic identification, analysis, and assessment of technical and operational weaknesses
  • Risk Assessment and Analysis Methodologies: Quantitative and qualitative approaches, risk modeling, and assessment frameworks
  • Risk Identification Techniques: Asset-based, threat-based, vulnerability-based, and scenario-based risk identification approaches
  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): Understanding potential impacts of security incidents on business operations, processes, and revenue
  • Risk Context and Environment: Internal and external factors influencing risk assessment and organizational risk profile

B. Information Security Risk Response

  • Risk Treatment and Response Options: Accept, avoid, transfer, or mitigate strategies with cost-benefit analysis
  • Risk and Control Ownership: Defining clear accountability, responsibility assignment, and governance structures for risk management
  • Risk Monitoring and Reporting: Continuous monitoring processes, KRIs, risk dashboards, and stakeholder communication
  • Risk Appetite and Tolerance Definition: Establishing acceptable risk levels, tolerance thresholds, and risk criteria
  • Control Selection and Implementation: Choosing appropriate risk treatment measures and implementing compensating controls
  • Risk Communication and Documentation: Risk registers, risk statements, and communication to various stakeholder levels

üéØ Key Supporting Tasks

  • Participate in and/or oversee the risk identification, risk assessment and risk treatment process
  • Participate in and/or oversee the vulnerability assessment and threat analysis process
  • Identify, recommend or implement appropriate risk treatment and response options to manage risk to acceptable levels
  • Determine whether information security controls are appropriate and effectively manage risk to an acceptable level
  • Facilitate the integration of information risk management into business and IT processes
  • Monitor for internal and external factors that may require reassessment of risk
  • Report on information security risk, including noncompliance and changes in information risk, to key stakeholders
  • Establish and maintain risk management processes that support business objectives and regulatory requirements
  • Develop and maintain risk assessment methodologies and frameworks appropriate to the organization
  • Coordinate risk management activities across different business units and functions

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Program Management focuses on translating security strategy into operational reality through program development, implementation, and management. It encompasses the practical aspects of building and maintaining effective security programs that deliver measurable results.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

  • Information Asset Identification and Classification: Data classification schemes, asset inventory management, data lifecycle, and protection requirements
  • Industry Standards and Frameworks: Implementation of security frameworks (NIST, ISO 27001/27002, CIS Controls) and industry best practices
  • Information Security Policies, Procedures and Guidelines: Development, maintenance, and communication of comprehensive security documentation
  • Information Security Program Metrics: KPIs, measurement frameworks, performance indicators, and program effectiveness assessment

Information Security Incident Management

30% of Exam

This domain provides comprehensive training in incident management, including preparedness, response, recovery, and continuous improvement processes for organizational resilience.

üìä Domain Overview

Information Security Incident Management encompasses all activities related to preparing for, responding to, and recovering from security incidents. It ensures organizational resilience through effective incident response capabilities and continuous improvement of security posture.

üéØ Sub-domains and Topics

A. Incident Management Readiness

  • Incident Response Plan: Comprehensive planning for incident response activities, procedures, escalation paths, and coordination mechanisms
  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): Understanding critical business functions, dependencies, impact assessment, and recovery prioritization
  • Business Continuity Plan (BCP): Ensuring business operations continuity during and after incidents, alternative processes, and operational resilience
  • Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP): Restoring IT systems and operations after incidents, recovery procedures, and technical restoration processes
  • Incident Classification and Categorization: Systematic approach to incident triage, severity levels, priority assignment, and escalation criteria
  • Incident Management Training, Testing and Evaluation: Preparedness through tabletop exercises, simulations, training programs, and plan validation
  • Incident Response Team Structure: Team organization, roles and responsibilities, skill requirements, and communication protocols

B. Incident Management Operations

  • Incident Management Tools and Techniques: Technology platforms, automation tools, forensic tools, and incident tracking systems
  • Incident Investigation and Evaluation: Forensic analysis, evidence preservation, root cause analysis, and impact assessment procedures
  • Incident Containment Methods: Strategies to limit incident impact, prevent spread, isolation techniques, and damage control measures
  • Incident Response Communications: Internal and external communication protocols, stakeholder notification, media relations, and regulatory reporting
  • Incident Eradication and Recovery: Removing threats, system restoration, data recovery, and return to normal operations
  • Post-Incident Review Practices: Lessons learned, process improvement, corrective actions, and knowledge transfer
  • Legal and Regulatory Compliance: Evidence handling, chain of custody, regulatory notifications, and legal coordination

üéØ Key Supporting Tasks

  • Establish and maintain an incident response plan, in alignment with the business continuity plan and disaster recovery plan
  • Establish and maintain an information security incident classification and categorization process
  • Develop and implement processes to ensure the timely identification of information security incidents
  • Establish and maintain processes to investigate and document information security incidents in accordance with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Establish and maintain incident handling process, including containment, notification, escalation, eradication and recovery
  • Organize, train, equip and assign responsibilities to incident response teams
  • Establish and maintain incident communication plans and processes for internal and external parties
  • Evaluate incident management plans through testing and review, including table-top exercises, checklist review and simulation testing at planned intervals
  • Conduct post-incident reviews to facilitate continuous improvement, including root-cause analysis, lessons learned, corrective actions and reassessment of risk
  • Coordinate incident response activities across multiple business units, external parties, and regulatory bodies
  • Maintain incident response capabilities and readiness through regular training, testing, and capability assessments
